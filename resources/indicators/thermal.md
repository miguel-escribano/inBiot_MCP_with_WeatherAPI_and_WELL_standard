# InBiot Thermal Comfort Indicator

**Source:** https://www.inbiot.es/wikinbiot/thermohygrometric-comfort-indicator

## Overview

The Thermal Comfort Indicator (0-100) evaluates thermohygrometric conditions in indoor environments based on temperature and humidity measurements.

## Calculation Method

This indicator combines:
- **Temperature measurements** - Optimal range considerations
- **Relative humidity levels** - Comfort and health implications
- **Combined effect** - How temperature and humidity interact for occupant comfort

## Score Interpretation

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 80-100 | Excellent | Optimal conditions for productivity and wellbeing |
| 60-79 | Good | Generally comfortable conditions |
| 40-59 | Acceptable | Minor comfort issues may be present |
| 20-39 | Poor | Occupants likely to experience discomfort |
| 0-19 | Very Poor | Significant comfort issues requiring attention |
